ChronoServ

An interactive fiction by Andrew Fraser

M1

Hello? Is this ChronoServ? My return transport isn't working. I've been in 1987 for six days now. I'm in a bedsit in one of the Hulme Crescents — third floor, damp on the ceiling, a mattress on the floor. Someone downstairs has been playing Whitney Houston's "I Wanna Dance with Somebody" since breakfast. Through the other wall I can hear The Smiths. Through the floor, something with a lot of bass that I don't recognize. The whole building sounds like three different decades arguing.

Outside my window there's a concrete balcony and then just Manchester, going on and on in the rain.

The last thing I remember working was the concert. New Order at the Haçienda, June 10th. They closed with "Temptation." Then I went outside and everything went wrong.

Please help.

Choose your tone:

A) Stay calm, be professional. B) Make it clear this is urgent. C) Demand a supervisor immediately.

M3

I know you said to stay calm but I'm sleeping in the Hulme Crescents on a mattress that smells like someone else's decade. The television gets four channels and one of them is in Welsh. Margaret Thatcher just won another election — I watched it happen on the other three channels simultaneously and everyone in the building started shouting. Someone threw a boot at the wall. I don't know whose boot it was. I don't know whose wall it was.

Downstairs has switched from Whitney Houston to Pet Shop Boys. "It's a Sin." Over and over. The whole Crescent smells like someone's cooking something with too much cumin and not enough ventilation.

I had a kebab for dinner. Eighty pence. It was fine. Everything is fine. Nothing is fine.

A) Take a breath. Try again, politely. B) Get more upset. C) Exaggerate the danger to seem more urgent.

M4

I have been "flagged." I don't know what that means. I am a paying customer who is stuck thirty-seven years before my birth and I have been flagged. Yesterday I walked to the newsagent and bought an NME and a Melody Maker just to have something to read. The NME says The Smiths are breaking up. Johnny Marr's gone. Everyone in Manchester is acting like someone died. The woman in the corner shop said, "Well, that's it then, isn't it?" and I didn't know what to say because I know it only gets worse from here.

A) Apologize. Start over. B) Threaten legal action. C) "I want to speak to someone who actually knows what a temporal anchor is."

M5

Cal from Tech Support is asking what happened at the concert. You were there for New Order's last gig at the Haçienda — Factory Records' own club, Tony Wilson's place. The room was moving. They played "Bizarre Love Triangle" and "Age of Consent" and finished with "Temptation" and for three hours you forgot you were from anywhere else.

Then you went outside. And it all went wrong.

A) Tell the truth. All of it. B) Tell most of the truth. Skip the part about after the concert. C) "What would hypothetically happen if someone hypothetically spoke to someone they hypothetically shouldn't have?"

M6

You've reached ChronoServ Billing. An automated system. In 1987. Via post. You wonder if somewhere in 2045 there's a room full of people hand-writing these letters and sending them back through time via second-class Royal Mail.

A) "I'd like my account details, please." B) "I want to dispute the charges for a trip that has stranded me in the Thatcher era." C) "What exactly does my warranty cover?"

M7

A different agent. Not Cal. The message arrived at 3am their time — you can tell from the timestamp. The handwriting is different. Smaller. Faster. The ink is blue where Cal's was black, and the letters lean forward like they're in a hurry to get somewhere.

Their name is Sable.

A) Tell them everything. The whole story. B) "Is there a workaround? Hypothetically?" C) "Forget the anchor for a second. How's your night going?"

M8

Cal is running diagnostics on your anchor. He's sent you a readout that looks like a heart monitor for a dying clock. He's circled something in red and written "this is bad" with an arrow. Below that, someone else — different handwriting, blue ink — has added: "understatement."

A) "Can you pull up my anchor's calibration specs?" B) "How long would a physical rescue take?" C) "The readout says 'temporal deviation.' What does that mean?"

M9

Sable asked about the concert. You told them about the concert — the heat inside the Haçienda, the sound, the way the bass went through the floor and into your chest. Then Sable asked about after. You told them about after, too. About stepping outside. About the rain. About the stranger.

Now it's quiet. The kind of quiet that happens in letters when someone is choosing their next sentence carefully.

It's 2am. The building has gone still except for someone on the ground floor playing Echo and the Bunnymen's "The Killing Moon" so quietly you can barely hear it through the concrete. Just the bassline. Just the pulse.

A) Tell Sable about the stranger. Really tell them. B) "Help me. Really help me. Off the record." C) "Thanks for talking. I'll figure it out. New ticket tomorrow." D) "I think you might be the only real person I've talked to in weeks. Both sides of the time gap."

M10 — THE FINAL MESSAGE

You have:

  • An override code.
  • An anchor serial number.
  • A calibration frequency.

You'll also need to prove where and when you are. The system is going to ask you something only someone there would know. Think about the concert. Think about what you heard.

If you're ready, go to the reset form. If you're missing something, return to the menu.

M11 — DEAD END

Your ticket has been suspended. The bedsit is quiet.

Outside, Manchester carries on. Someone on the ground floor is having a party — you can hear the bass through the concrete, four floors of it, and someone has propped the stairwell door open so the smoke drifts up. At the newsagent this morning, the front page of the Manchester Evening News said something about the Channel Tunnel. It will take six years to build. You might still be here when it opens.

The radio in the kebab shop is playing "The Killing Moon." The owner is singing along, quietly, while he shaves meat off the spit. Eighty pence. Same as yesterday. Same as tomorrow.

(Your ticket suspension lasts seven days. After that, you may submit a new request.)

A) Wait a week. Submit a new ticket. B) Wait a week. Wander around Manchester. Think about your choices.

M12 — THE HONEST PATH

You told Sable everything and Sable was kind about it. Genuinely kind. But the extraction costs £2,347,891.00. Adjusted for temporal exchange rates. Non-negotiable.

Outside the post office window, a busker is playing "How Soon Is Now?" on an acoustic guitar, badly. A woman walks past with a Woolworths bag. A bus goes by advertising the new Levi's advert — the one with the bloke in the laundrette. Everything is ordinary and thirty-seven years away from where you belong.

A) Accept the charges. You'll figure it out when you get home. B) "There has to be another way." C) You can't afford it. You can't afford anything. Put the pager down.
